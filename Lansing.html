About Lansing

Lansing, the capital city of Michigan, is at the head of the state's government and has evolved into an indispensable hub for education, healthcare, and industry. Michigan State University is located in the neighboring East Lansing; the city benefits from extensive research, education, and innovation. As the political heartbeat of Michigan, Lansing houses the State Capitol, the Supreme Court, and numerous government offices. The economy is diverse, bolstered by automobile manufacturing, healthcare, insurance, and education sectors.

Downtown Lansing has been revitalized with new residential and commercial developments, complementing its historic government buildings. The city hosts events like Silver Bells in the City and the Common Ground Music Festival, which draw large crowds annually. Neighborhoods like Old Town and REO Town are rich in history and character. Lansing offers scenic outdoor spots like the Lansing River Trail and Potter Park Zoo.

Lansing Trivia

  • Lansing became the state capital in 1847, before being incorporated as a city in 1859.
  • The Michigan State Capitol was built in 1879 and is a National Historic Landmark.
  • Lansing is home to Michigan State University, one of the largest universities in the U.S.
  • Potter Park Zoo in Lansing is Michigan's oldest public zoo, founded in 1920.
